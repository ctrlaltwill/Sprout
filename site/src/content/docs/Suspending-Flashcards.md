title: "Suspending Flashcards"

Last modified: 30/03/2026

## Purpose

Suspending removes a flashcard from study sessions until you unsuspend it.

The flashcard is kept in your collection, but it is not shown for review.

Unlike burying, suspension does not reset automatically.

## Suspend Steps

### During a study session

- Press **`S`** on your keyboard, or
- Open the **More menu** (press `M` or tap **...**) and choose **Suspend**.

### From the Flashcard Library

1. Open the [Flashcard Library](../Flashcard-Library).
2. Select one or more flashcards.
3. Click the **Suspend** button in the bulk actions bar.

To unsuspend, select suspended flashcards and click **Unsuspend**.

## Find Suspended

In [Flashcard Library](../Flashcard-Library), filter by Stage -> Suspended.

## Use Cases

Suspending is useful when:

- A flashcard is outdated or incorrect and you want to stop reviewing it until you fix the content.
- You're studying a subset of material (for example only certain chapters) and want to temporarily exclude other flashcards.
- A flashcard is a leech and you need to rewrite it before continuing.

## Compare Bury

| | Bury | Suspend |
|--|------|---------|
| **Duration** | 24 hours (automatic) | Until you manually unsuspend |
| **Best for** | Skipping a flashcard for the rest of the day | Removing a flashcard from reviews long-term |
| **Scheduling impact** | None | None |
| **How to reverse** | Automatic (after 24 hours) | Manual (unsuspend in library or during study) |

> [!TIP]
> Use **Bury** for "I'll try again tomorrow" and **Suspend** for "I need to fix this flashcard first" or "I don't want to see this again".
